# California's New Law Targeting Loud Streaming Ads

As of July 1, California is implementing an innovative legal framework aimed at addressing the issue of loud streaming advertisements. This law seeks to enhance user experience while contributing to the establishment of new standards in the digital media landscape. The mandatory regulations for publishers target overly loud ads that distract viewers. Such a reform is expected to provide users with a more comfortable viewing experience and necessitate the development of new strategies for advertisers.

In the aftermath of this law's initial implementation, content creators and streamers will need to reassess their tactics for capturing audience attention. Eliminating the technical factors that lead to loud ad contracts could lead to a transformative change in the advertising industry and potentially reduce viewer disengagement.

Particularly with the assistance of AI and data analytics, brands may find themselves compelled to produce quieter yet engaging content. The changes initiated in California could resonate across other states and countries, paving the way for the evolution of global streaming ad standards.
