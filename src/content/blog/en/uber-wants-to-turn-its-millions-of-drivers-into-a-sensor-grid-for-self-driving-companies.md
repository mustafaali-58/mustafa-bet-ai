# Uber Aims to Transform Its Drivers into a Sensor Grid for Self-Driving Companies

In a recent interview at TechCrunch's StrictlyVC event in San Francisco, Uber's Chief Technology Officer, Praveen Neppalli Naga, detailed an ambitious new initiative aimed at harnessing driver data to enhance the development of autonomous vehicles. This strategy represents a natural evolution of a nascent program named 'AV Labs' that the company announced in late January.

Uber plans to leverage the sensor data collected from its vast network of drivers to construct a more reliable and efficient infrastructure for autonomous vehicles. Such innovative solutions not only have the potential to create a smoother urban traffic experience but also to provide a safer driving environment. Furthermore, the objective is to gain a competitive edge in the rapidly growing autonomous vehicle market.

This initiative aligns well with advancements in technologies like data analytics and machine learning. By establishing a sensor grid from driver data, Uber will not only expand its operational model but also facilitate a faster and more trustworthy rollout of self-driving technology. This forward-looking approach positions Uber at the forefront of the autonomous vehicle revolution.
