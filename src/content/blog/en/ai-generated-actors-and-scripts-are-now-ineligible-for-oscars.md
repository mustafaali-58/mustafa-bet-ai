# AI-Generated Actors and Scripts Ineligible for Oscars

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence technologies is significantly transforming the cinema industry. Yet, these changes are also influencing the criteria for prestigious awards like the Oscars. The Academy has announced that AI-generated actors and scripts can no longer qualify for nominations.

This decision raises questions about the place of AI-generated content within the creative process. Artists like Tilly Norwood, who utilize AI in content creation, are poised to face negative repercussions from this announcement. The Academy asserts that AI should not be recognized as a creative entity, emphasizing that the encouragement of human creativity and imagination should remain paramount.

Looking ahead, we will witness how cinema pieces evolve through collaboration between AI and humans. However, it is clear that the contributions of AI must be more clearly delineated in the process. AI-driven content is not only pushing the boundaries of creativity, but it is also prompting a reexamination of industry norms. The balance between art and technology will undeniably be a topic of greater discussion in the coming years.
