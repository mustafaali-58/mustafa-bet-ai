# Infrastructure for the Next Generation of Robots: Jean-Baptiste Kempf's Kyber Project

French entrepreneur and open-source legend Jean-Baptiste Kempf is transitioning from enhancing free video player experiences to focusing on the development of infrastructure for robots. His project, Kyber, is designed as an infrastructure layer for the real-time control of remote devices.

Kyber’s framework allows for smoother and more efficient management of robots and other remotely controlled systems. This approach holds great potential not only for industrial applications but also for robotic systems utilized in everyday life.

As part of this development, the power of the open-source community is leveraged, enabling users to integrate such systems more quickly and easily. Kempf brings his past experiences and open-source philosophy into this new endeavor, making him a fascinating figure in the tech world.

In conclusion, the potential of Kyber in the realm of robotics could revolutionize the future technology ecosystem. Under Kempf’s leadership, this innovation is sure to enhance the way users interact with AI and robotic technologies.
