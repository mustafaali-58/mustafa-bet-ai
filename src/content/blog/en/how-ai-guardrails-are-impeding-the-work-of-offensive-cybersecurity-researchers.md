# How AI Guardrails Are Impeding the Work of Offensive Cybersecurity Researchers

In recent years, artificial intelligence (AI) systems have begun to play a significant role in various sectors, including cybersecurity. However, the guardrails developed by companies like OpenAI and Anthropic are making it increasingly difficult for some cybersecurity researchers to carry out their work. In this blog post, we will delve into how these protective measures impact defensive cybersecurity research and the potential challenges they pose.

Cybersecurity researchers are focused on discovering unknown vulnerabilities and developing tools to exploit them. However, the protection mechanisms embedded within AI systems may significantly lessen the effectiveness of these investigations. Findings from various studies indicate that these guardrails restrict the scope of defensive research and complicate the acquisition of knowledge regarding potential threats.

Researchers express that due to the AI's restrictions on prohibited or harmful content, they are forced to operate within a much more limited domain. This situation adversely affects both their capabilities to detect cyber threats and their ability to devise suitable solutions against such threats.

Many experts argue that there is a pressing need to reassess the AI systems' guardrails in a more flexible and research-friendly manner. The ongoing tension between such measures and the needs of the cybersecurity community is critically important for the efficiency of future research. Continuously fostering communication and collaboration between researchers and AI developers can not only aid the evolution of security perception, but also unveil pathways to counter new threats.
